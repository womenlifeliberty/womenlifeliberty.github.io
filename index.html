Mahsa (Zhina) Amini

Mahsa, a 22-year-old Iranian-Kurdish woman, died on September 16, 2022, after falling into a coma while in custody of the Iranian "Morality Police". Three days earlier, police had stopped and arrested Mahsa for not wearing her hijab, "properly". Mahsa's family state that her death was caused by injuries to her head-- something that the Iranian government rejects.
Mahsa's death has sparked widespread protests inside and outside Iran. The Iranian government has limited-- and in many areas completely shut down-- internet access. This has made it impossible to provide a first-hand and impartial view into ongoing crackdowns on protests led by the Iranian women.

Never Forget Them

We are observing a unique time in the history of Iran-- and maybe humankind. The current movement in Iran-- led by young women, students, high school girls-- has been under brutal attack by the Iranian security forces.

These forces have committed monstrous crimes; they attacked high schools, took children as hostages, and killed tens of children. We lost our fearless heroes to monstrous acts of a cowardly regime. Nevertheless, these young women who shared their lives and dreams generously, changed us forever.

Protests In Iran

As of today, widespread protests have flared up many Iranian cities and rural areas. These self-organized protests have been started since Sept 16th and have humiliated the Iranian regime. There have been many global rallies in support of the regime change and in solidarity with the victims of these protests. Initiated by youth, current uprisings is now being supported by many in the working class. Growing number of sectors, e.g. oil & gas, refineries, manufacturing, etc. have stood by the women and people of Iran by going on nationwide strikes.
